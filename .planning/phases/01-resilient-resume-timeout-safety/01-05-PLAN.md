<objective>
Close the two compound defects that make Plan 01-03's `PARALLEL_JOB_TIMEOUT_SECONDS` enforcement silently disabled under `--quiet` (CR-02) and ineffective at stopping the underlying tool (CR-03). Both fixes live in `lib/parallel.sh` and share the same change site, so they are bundled into one plan rather than artificially split.

Purpose: 01-VERIFICATION.md marked SC3 PARTIAL with two compound defects.
- **CR-02 (Gap B):** The timeout-kill check sits inside `if [[ ${PARALLEL_MODE} == true ]] && [[ ${OUTPUT_VERBOSITY:-1} -ge 1 ]] ...` at `lib/parallel.sh:469` and `:579`. Under `--quiet` (OUTPUT_VERBOSITY=0) the entire while-loop is skipped — no timeout-kill runs. The cfg comment at `reconftw.cfg:314-317` explicitly recommends `PARALLEL_JOB_TIMEOUT_SECONDS=600` for CI, and CI runs are precisely where `--quiet` is used → the feature is silently disabled in its documented primary use case.
- **CR-03 (Gap C):** `_timeout_kill_job` at `lib/parallel.sh:53-77` signals the wrapper subshell PID stored in `batch_pids[$idx]` (the `$!` of the `(...) >$log_file &` at `:438`). SIGTERM to a Bash subshell does NOT auto-propagate to its children. Verified empirically: the wrapper dies but `sleep 30` (acting as a stand-in for puredns/dnsx/ffuf) keeps running orphaned to PID 1. On axiom-distributed runs the remote workers also keep going because the local `axiom-scan` wrapper is the orphan path.

Both defects undermine SC3's headline guarantee ("terminates any single `parallel_funcs` child exceeding 10 minutes via kill -TERM, allowing the batch to continue") — the BATCH does continue, but the actual TOOL doesn't terminate. This plan closes both.

Output:
- `lib/parallel.sh`: decoupled gate at the two heartbeat-loop entry conditions (`:469` and `:579`). The loop runs when (timeout is enabled) OR (verbosity wants UI). Heartbeat snapshots fire only on the verbosity arm; timeout enforcement fires unconditionally inside the loop body.
- `lib/parallel.sh`: new private helper `_kill_tree` (declared above `_timeout_kill_job` near the existing `_throttle_jobs` at :33-42) that walks the process tree via `pgrep -P` and signals each descendant. Falls back to wrapper-only kill if `pgrep` is unavailable (CLAUDE.md macOS compatibility).
- `lib/parallel.sh`: `_timeout_kill_job` body updated to call `_kill_tree $pid TERM`, poll up to `PARALLEL_KILL_GRACE_SECONDS`, then `_kill_tree $pid KILL` if still alive. The persistence writes (FAIL + reason=timeout) and `log_json` call remain unchanged — only the kill mechanism is replaced.
- `reconftw.cfg`: NO changes (the existing comment at `:317` is already accurate post-fix; D-11 default `0` and D-13 grace `10` remain locked).

No new tests; Phase 4 (TEST-01) covers parallel_funcs timeout-kill regression coverage per CONTEXT.md §Claude's Discretion.
